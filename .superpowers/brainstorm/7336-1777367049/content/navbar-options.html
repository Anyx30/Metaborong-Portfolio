A — Simple Dropdown (Recommended)

Clean top-level nav: Services ▾ / Work / About / Blog / Let's Talk. Services opens a dropdown showing the 3 pillar categories, each linking to the hub page. Visitors who want specifics click into a category.

Pros

  • Clean, uncluttered bar — 5 items max
  • Categories are scannable in one glance
  • Standard pattern founders expect

Cons

  • Individual services not visible without hover

B — Mega Menu

Services opens a full-width panel showing all 14 services across 3 columns. Every service is visible on hover — great for return visitors who know what they want.

Pros

  • Full service catalogue visible at a glance
  • No extra clicks to find a specific service

Cons

  • Can feel overwhelming to cold visitors
  • More complex to build responsively on mobile

Each pillar is a top-level nav item with its own dropdown → individual services

C — Split Pillar Nav

Each pillar (Web3, AI Agents, Product Studio) is its own nav item, each with its own dropdown. No "Services" umbrella — the pillars are the navigation.

Pros

  • Pillars get equal visual weight
  • Signals 3 distinct offerings clearly

Cons

  • 7+ nav items — too crowded on mobile
  • Buries Work, About, Blog

The navbar is what visitors see — URL structure stays invisible. Click your preference.
