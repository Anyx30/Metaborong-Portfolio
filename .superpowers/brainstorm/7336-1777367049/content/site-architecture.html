A — Flat (Current approach)

All services on one page with anchor links. What the site does today.

Pros

  • Fastest to build
  • Simple navigation

Cons

  • One URL can't rank for 14 different keywords
  • No page depth = no SEO value
  • Dead end for content strategy

B — Category Hub + Individual Pages (Recommended)

Three pillar hub pages (Web3, AI Agents, Product Studio) each containing individual service sub-pages. Matches the strategy doc's pillar/cluster model exactly.

Pros

  • Every service gets its own URL + keyword target
  • Hub pages rank for category terms ("web3 development company")
  • Internal linking flows naturally pillar → cluster → blog
  • Scalable — add services without restructuring

Cons

  • More pages to write (14 service pages + 3 hubs)
  • Needs a clear nav to not overwhelm visitors

/blog/ /work/ /about/ /contact/

C — All Individual Pages, Flat Root

Every service gets its own top-level URL, no hub categories. Simpler URL structure, but loses the pillar authority that hub pages provide.

Pros

  • Short, clean URLs per service
  • Good for individual keyword targeting

Cons

  • No hub page to rank for category-level terms
  • Internal linking harder to organize
  • Navigation becomes cluttered with 14 top-level items
Approach B gives us:   3 hub pages + 14 service pages + homepage + about + work + blog + contact  = ~20 core pages + blog posts
